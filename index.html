computer parts

By: Ian Wood

Parts of a Computer:

  • Power supply
  • Core Processing Unit (CPU)
  • Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)
  • Motherboard
  • Memory (RAM)
  • Hard drives
  • Case

What Each Part Does:

Power Supply

The power suppy is the hardware responsible for powering the computer.
Without a power supply, a computer won't even turn on. If you are looking
to build a pc on a budget, a good choice wood be the EVGA 500w. For a more
advanced pc, any of the modular Corsair power supplies would be ideal, as
they are some of the best on the market.

CPU

The CPU is sort of the brains of the computer. It handles most of the
complex processes that happen within the computer. For instance, without
a cpu, no mouse or keyboard clicks would even be registered and the
computer would basically be rendered completely useless. A good cpu would
either be the Intel i5 6700k or the AMD Ryzen 1600.

GPU

In terms of gaming pc's, the gpu is probably the most important part.
The GPU is responsible for the processing of all graphical functions
taking place within games and other processes. A good gpu for a budget
would be the AMD Radeon RX 480 or Nvidia GTX 1060. I really good higher
end gpu would definitely be the GTX 1080.

Motherboard

For a novice pc builder, the motherboard of a pc is quite a sight to behold
because of how complicated it looks. To put it simple, all a motherboard does
is route the power from the power supply to all the parts of the pc. It's
impossible to reccomend a single motherboard because it really depends on what
cpu you have. A good motherboard brand, however, is Gigabyte or Asus.

Memory

The memory is probably the easiest part of a pc for a beginner to understand.
It basically stores information that you are using right now, like how many
tabs you have open or what you are writing in word dcument to be later
put into your hard drives. All RAM is basicall the same, but make sure
to get at least 8 GB of it.

Hard drives

Hard drives are where all of your information is stored. There are two types of
Drives: Solid State Drives (SSD) or or just regular hard drives. SSD's are much
faster than hard drives but they are much more expensive. On budget, most people
can't afford an ssd, but if you can afford one, ssd's are great for boot drives.
Gaming pcs also need at least a terabyte of space.

Case

The case for a pc is very simple. It basically just holds all of the parts together
in one place. Without a case, all the parts would be open to the elements and it
would look pretty ugly. The case is the main thing that can either make a pc look
super ugly or super sexy. NZXT and Corsair are both good PC case brands.
